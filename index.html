I've spent my career at the intersection of complex, distributed systems (and most recently AI systems and infrastructure) and the unglamorous, essential work of turning them into products in the hands of real users. That means building the teams, systems, and culture that can turn a research paper or prototype into a product experience at a global scale.

At Apple, I was a Senior Director of Engineering in AI/ML, focused on turning advanced ML research into products used by hundreds of millions of people. I spent time on Siri, Search, and World Knowledge.

Before Apple, I led a 400+ engineer organization at Netflix responsible for search, personalization, and ML platform for 230M+ members. Before that, I spent four years at Slack scaling infrastructure through hypergrowth — from 10 engineers to 100+, and from $105M to $401M in revenue. I've also co-founded two companies, which taught me more about shipping than anything else.

My research background is in distributed systems and human-computer interaction. I published eight peer-reviewed papers at IBM Almaden Research. Old habits die hard: I still think about systems at the level of how humans and machines actually interact.

I have a BS with Honors and an MS in Computer Science from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. I started programming on a Commodore 64 when I was 14.

I'm a runner, a chef, a programmer, and a parent, and I love figuring out how things work.
